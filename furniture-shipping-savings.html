Large Package Surcharges

Any box with a longest dimension over 96 inches, or combined length + girth over 130 inches, triggers a large package fee automatically. For furniture, that's nearly every shipment.

Additional Handling Fees

Irregular shapes, non-conveyable items, or packages exceeding standard weight thresholds trigger additional handling charges. Furniture hits this on most SKUs.

$15–$30 per package

Residential Delivery Premiums

DTC furniture brands ship to residential addresses on virtually every order. Residential delivery surcharges stack on top of all other fees.

Furniture often ships in non-standard box dimensions. DIM weight pricing means you're paying for cubic space, not actual weight.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the large package surcharge actually negotiable?

Yes — it's one of the most negotiable categories in a carrier contract for furniture shippers. Most brands have never asked because their carrier rep has no incentive to bring it up. Chris has pre-negotiated rates in this category specifically for furniture and home goods profiles.

Our carrier gave us a 40% discount — why would there still be room to negotiate?

Because the discount applies to your base rates, not your surcharges. Large package, additional handling, and residential delivery premiums are separate line items — and they're often set at default carrier rates regardless of how much volume you ship.

Will this disrupt our current operations or fulfillment?

No. In most cases, the only operational change is a carrier transition (from UPS to FedEx or vice versa). We manage that process completely. Your fulfillment team keeps doing exactly what they're doing — just with lower per-shipment costs.

How fast can we expect to see impact?

For furniture brands shipping at scale, initial savings from pre-negotiated rate categories can hit within days of implementation. For a company spending $300K/year on shipping, that's often $45K–$75K returned to margin annually.
