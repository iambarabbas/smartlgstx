Frequently Asked Questions

What makes fitness equipment a strong fit for shipping savings?

Fitness equipment — treadmills, weight racks, rowing machines, exercise bikes — is heavy, oversized, and shipped predominantly to residential addresses. This combination hits every major surcharge category: large package fees, additional handling charges, and residential delivery premiums on virtually every order.

Can you help a fitness equipment brand that ships both to gyms and direct-to-consumer?

Yes. We build rate structures around your actual shipment profile. Gym deliveries (commercial addresses) and direct-to-consumer shipments (residential) carry different surcharge treatments, and we negotiate each separately.

Do fitness equipment brands need to worry about peak season surcharges?

Yes — January is the highest-volume month for most fitness equipment brands, coinciding with New Year demand. UPS and FedEx both apply peak season surcharges during high-volume periods, which can significantly amplify costs exactly when fitness brands ship the most.

We import fitness equipment — can you help with international shipping costs?

Yes. Fitness equipment is commonly imported via major parcel carriers, and inbound international contracts are included in our audit. Clearance fees, international additional handling charges, and international fuel surcharges are all reviewable. We show you what's recoverable before you commit to anything.
