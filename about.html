Chris Tweedy — Founder & CEO

Chris Tweedy spent 13 years at UPS in sales and customer solutions. During that time, he sat across the table from thousands of shipping negotiations — on the carrier's side.

He knew which line items companies fought for. He knew which concessions UPS was willing to make — and which ones they'd never offer unless pushed. He knew which surcharge categories had real flexibility, and which carrier reps were bluffing.

He also knew that most businesses — even large, sophisticated ones — left significant money on the table in every negotiation. Not because they weren't smart. Because they didn't have someone who'd seen both sides.

After leaving UPS, Chris spent time as a shipping executive at a growth-stage company. Sitting on the buyer's side for the first time, he watched his own carrier rep use every tactic he'd once relied on.

He launched SmartLogistics with a simple thesis: companies deserve an expert who knows how carriers actually think.

Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly did you do at UPS for 13 years?

I was in sales and customer solutions — which means I sat across the table from businesses during carrier negotiations on UPS's side. I knew which concessions we were willing to make, which categories had flexibility, and which ones we'd never bring up unless a shipper pushed.

How is SmartLogistics different from other shipping consultants?

Most consultants work from benchmarking data — they compare your rates to industry averages. I worked inside the carrier for 13 years, which means I know the specific levers in a negotiation, not just the numbers. We also work exclusively on contingency — you pay nothing unless we save you money.

Why do you take a contingency fee instead of a flat retainer?

Because we only win when you win. A retainer model creates an incentive to bill hours regardless of outcome. A gain-share model means we're highly selective about which clients we take on — because our revenue depends on actually recovering savings.
