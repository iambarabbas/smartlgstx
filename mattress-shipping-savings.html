Frequently Asked Questions

Why do mattress-in-a-box brands get hit with especially high surcharges?

Mattresses — even compressed in a box — are among the most surcharge-dense shipments the major carriers process. They trigger large package surcharges (oversized dimensions), additional handling fees (non-conveyable, irregular shape), residential delivery premiums (nearly all DTC), and often oversize penalties on top.

What is an additional handling fee and how often does it apply to mattress shipments?

An additional handling fee is charged by the major carriers on packages that require special handling in the carrier network — typically items that are too heavy, too large, or irregular in shape. For compressed mattresses, this fee applies on virtually every shipment. At $29–$58 per package, it's one of the most consistent and negotiable surcharge categories.

Can Smart LGSTX help a mattress brand that ships high volumes nationally?

Yes — high-volume, nationally distributed mattress brands are among Smart LGSTX's strongest fits. The higher your shipment volume, the greater the compounding impact of even small per-unit surcharge reductions. A $5 reduction in large package surcharge across 10,000 annual shipments is $50,000 in recovered savings.

How does the carrier transition affect our fulfilment operations?

Smart LGSTX manages the carrier transition completely. The handoff — typically between carriers — is coordinated to avoid any gap in your fulfilment operations. Most clients continue shipping without interruption throughout the transition period.

We import mattress components — can you audit our international shipping too?

Yes. If you're importing foam, fabrics, or other components via major parcel carriers, your inbound international shipping is included in the audit. International contracts often get less attention during negotiations — clearance entry fees, inbound processing fees, and international additional handling charges all have room to move for companies with consistent import volume.
