FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I realistically save on carrier shipping?

Most clients save 10–20% of their total annual carrier spend. For specific surcharge categories — large package fees, additional handling, and residential delivery surcharges — individual reductions of 15–25% are common. For a company spending $200K/year on shipping, that typically means $20K–$40K back annually, with initial savings visible within the first week.

What is a carrier contract audit and why do I need one?

A carrier contract audit is a line-by-line review of every charge and rate category in your UPS or FedEx agreement. The average carrier contract has 250+ line items. Most businesses have only negotiated a handful of them. An audit identifies the categories where you're overpaying — large package surcharges, dimensional weight divisors, accessorial fees — and builds the case for renegotiation.

What are accessorial fees and why do they matter?

Accessorial fees are add-on charges that carriers apply on top of base shipping rates. They include large package surcharges, additional handling fees, residential delivery surcharges, delivery area surcharges, and peak season penalties. For companies shipping heavy or bulky products, accessorial fees often represent more than the base rate — and they're the least-negotiated part of most carrier contracts.

Is Smart LGSTX a broker, a consultant, or a negotiator?

Smart LGSTX is a shipping optimization service — not a broker. We don't earn commissions from carriers. We start with a free audit of your current carrier pricing, identify where you're overpaying, and implement savings. Our team has 50+ years of combined experience on the carrier side — which means we know what's negotiable and what your rep will never bring to the table on their own.
