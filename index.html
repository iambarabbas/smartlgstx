UPS and FedEx Are Not Your Partners. They're Your Biggest Unmanaged Cost.

If your company spends $100K–$500K per year on UPS or FedEx, your carrier contract has 250+ line items. Your carrier rep negotiated maybe 15 of them with you.

The rest — large package surcharges, additional handling fees, residential delivery premiums, dimensional weight multipliers, peak season penalties — are sitting exactly where the carrier wants them.

Your rep's job is to maximize what you pay. Not minimize it.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does SmartLogistics get paid?

Gain-share only — we take a percentage of the savings we recover, billed quarterly. If we don't save you money, you owe us nothing. There is no retainer, no upfront fee, and no charge if we don't deliver.

We already negotiated our rates — can you still help?

Almost certainly yes. Base rates are just one of 250+ line items in a carrier contract. The categories most shippers have never negotiated — large package surcharges, additional handling fees, dimensional weight divisors, and residential delivery premiums — are where the majority of recoverable savings live.

How long until we see savings?

Initial savings using pre-negotiated rates can be in place in as little as 1 week. Full optimization across all contract line items typically takes 3–6 months.

Do I need to switch carriers?

In about 95% of cases, yes. Moving from FedEx to UPS or vice versa is typically required to access the best negotiated rates. We're always transparent about this before you commit — and we handle the transition completely.
